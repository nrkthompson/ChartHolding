OECD Country Data

This classification provides readers with the overall positions of the different economies (countries/provinces/states) from a staged reopening perspective, along with their reopening progress in terms of detailed activities.

Aimed at presenting a rough picture of the economies’ relative positions, the selection of activities and the division of levels are based on a synthesis of the common features of the governments’ reopening strategies at different stages. The overall reopening level is computed as the arithmetic mean of all the detailed activity levels.

The information utilized to develop this classification is mainly retrieved from government website releases and local media sources in those economies. Readers should look at this classification with the caveat that it presents the overall picture only, while possible sub-national or local variations exist.

Twitter Data

The Media Ecosystem has developed and curated a unique Twitter dataset that seeks to capture the totality of the Canadian Twitter conversation. To do so, we identified a set of politicians, political journalists, public intellectuals, and Canadian-specific hashtags and collected every Twitter user that touched one of the themes or individuals. We then evaluate each individual, looking at their follower or friending behaviour, biographies, self-identified location, and hashtag use and include them in our sample if we determine them to be algorithmically likely to be Canadian.

This iterative dataset of Twitter users continues to grow. As new users become identified through contact follower or friending behaviour, hashtag use, or use of keywords of interest, we evaluate and include them in our sample. This effort differs massively from frequently employed hashtag and elite-oriented collection efforts in that it provides visibility into the universe of Canadian Twitter and allows for over-time comparisons of activity, attention, sentiment, and other quantities of interest.

The topics shown are based on a dictionary approach run over a 10% sample of Canadian-identified tweets. Percentages shown reflect the proportion of the total number of tweets collected that day that contain one or more tweets associated with a particular topic.
