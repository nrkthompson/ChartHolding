On May 25, the Czech government proceeded to the fourth and final phase of its reopening plan, allowing all catering establishments, hotels, spas, and public swimming pools to resume business, subject to detailed sanitary guidelines.92 Mass gatherings of up to 300 people are being permitted in both indoor and outdoor settings, while professional and amateur athletes are being allowed to organize training with up to 300 participants.93 On June 5, Prime Minister Andrej Babis announced substantial changes to land border restrictions with a number of European countries.94 Czech citizens, as well temporary and permanent residents of the Czech Republic, will no longer have to undergo a mandatory quarantine period or submit a negative COVID-19 test upon entering the country.95 Citizens and residents of Hungary, Slovakia, Austria, and Germany - as well as EU citizens with a certificate of temporary or permanent residence in any of these countries - are also permitted to enter the republic without a mandatory quarantine period or negative COVID-19 test.96 Other activities and business operations are expected to resume throughout the month of June.97
