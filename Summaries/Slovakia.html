Slovakia

February 15, 2021

Slovakia reported 572 cases of COVID-19 on February 15, below its 7-day moving average of 2,024 cases per day. Forty-four of Slovakia’s 72 administrative regions are at level 3 out of 4 on the country’s regional alert scale, compared to forty-one last week. Level 3 permits the reopening of kindergartens and primary schools for in-person learning, as well as the resumption of many non-essential activities for residents who have had a negative COVID-19 test in the past 7 days.

February 8, 2021

Slovakia reported 552 cases of COVID-19 on February 7, below its 7-day moving average of 1,916 cases per day. From February 8, Slovakia will begin using a colour-coded regional alert system in order to establish localized public health guidelines. This is in addition to a similar national alert system already in use. Slovakia’s state of emergency was extended on February 8, with the country remaining at the most restrictive “black” phase of its national alert system. Forty-one of Slovakia’s 72 administrative regions will begin the week at level 3 out of 4 on the country’s regional alert scale, which permits the reopening of kindergartens and primary schools for in-person learning, as well as the resumption of many non-essential activities for residents who had a negative COVID-19 test in the past 7 days.

February 1, 2021

Slovakia reported 1,723 cases of COVID-19 on January 31, below its 7-day moving average of 2,062 cases per day. The country will remain in lockdown until February 7, with ongoing restrictions shuttering all non-essential establishments, including theatres, hairdressers, driving schools, and fitness centres. Only essential businesses, such as pharmacies and grocery stores, can remain open at this time. Gatherings remain limited to two people. As of February 1, Slovakians who can produce a negative COVID-19 test, or prove that they have received an approved vaccine, are exempt from the most restrictive lockdown measures, such as limitations on movement outside of the home during curfew hours.

January 25, 2021

Slovakia reported 1,905 cases of COVID-19 on January 24, a significant improvement from its single-day record of 6,315 new cases on December 31. The country will remain in lockdown until January 31, with ongoing restrictions shuttering all non-essential establishments, including theatres, hairdressers, driving schools, and fitness centres. Only essential businesses, such as pharmacies and grocery stores, can remain open at this time. All mass events, including those with fewer than 6 people, remain prohibited.

January 18, 2021

Slovakia reported 573 cases of COVID-19 on January 17, just over two weeks after setting the country’s single-day record of 6,315 new cases. The country will remain in lockdown until January 31, with ongoing restrictions shuttering all non-essential establishments, including theatres, hairdressers, driving schools, and fitness centres. Only essential businesses, such as pharmacies and grocery stores, can remain open at this time. All mass events, including those with fewer than 6 people, remain prohibited. Shoe repair shops were added to the country’s list of essential businesses on January 11.

December 21, 2020

Despite the reported success of SlovakiaÕs mass-testing and quarantine operation -- in which millions of Slovakians were tested for COVID-19 twice over a 3-week period -- the country is experiencing a major resurgence. A record 3,991 new cases were reported on December 20; the previous single-day record of 3,363 new cases was on October 30. New restrictions were introduced this week on the recommendation of SlovakiaÕs pandemic commission, with the current lockdown set to last until January 10, 2021. Under new restrictions, all commercial establishments must close, including theatres, hairdressers, driving schools, and fitness centres. Only essential businesses, such as pharmacies and grocery stores, can remain open. All mass events, including those with fewer than 6 people, are prohibited.

December 14, 2020

Despite the reported success of SlovakiaÕs mass-testing and quarantine operation -- in which millions of Slovakians were tested for COVID-19 twice over a 3-week period -- the country is experiencing a major resurgence of COVID-19 this week. It reported a record 3,707 new cases on December 12. This spike in cases comes as a surprise after nearly a month of declining single-day counts. In light of the countryÕs deteriorating epidemiological situation, SlovakiaÕs pandemic commission has proposed that lockdown restrictions be implemented once again. The proposed rules would be similar to those imposed after the first round of mass testing. This means that it would be possible to leave the house in order to shop for essentials and walk to school or work. The commission suggested that the lockdown be put in place until January 10, 2021. The proposal first needs to be approved by the cabinet. The health minister has stressed that it is necessary to impose the lockdown as soon as possible.

December 7, 2020

According to the Slovakian prime minister, the countryÕs mass-testing and quarantine operation -- in which millions of Slovakians were tested twice over a 3-week period -- has helped to cut the new infections in the country by more than half. With a total population of 5.5 million, Slovakia tested 3.6 million people during the first weekend of November and moved those testing positive into quarantine. Slovakia repeated the test for just over 2 million people last weekend, with a much lower infection rate.

November 30, 2020

As the spread of COVID-19 in Slovakia continues to slow, the countryÕs government is gradually easing restrictions in key areas. From November 27, 2020, public gatherings can take place in groups of up to 6 people -- provided that all participants have a negative RT-PCR or antigen test result within 12 hours. The second round of nationwide testing that was set to take place during the first weekend of December has been cancelled due to the fact that the country does not have enough antigen tests. The economy minister was assigned to purchase 16 million antigen tests; the first batch of four million tests is expected to be delivered by December 2.

November 23, 2020

Following the reportedly successful testing of over 80% of the countryÕs population, SlovakiaÕs government reopened theatres, cinemas and churches at half capacity on November 15 and allowed fitness centres and pools to resume operation with limited capacity. SlovakiaÕs ice hockey and soccer leagues also resumed without spectators on November 20. As the spread of COVID-19 in Slovakia continues to slow, the countryÕs prime minister has announced that his government will re-conduct nationwide mass testing in December based on the success of the initial run in Òcontaining the pandemic.Ó

November 16, 2020

While many European countries continue to lockdown as COVID-19 infections continue to rise, SlovakiaÕs government reopened theatres, cinemas and churches at half capacity on November 15. The country is also allowing fitness centres and pools to operate with limited capacity as of the same date. SlovakiaÕs ice hockey and soccer leagues will also restart, without spectators, on November 20. Slovakia reported 2,024 new infections on November 12 -- down from a record daily tally of more than 3,000 in late October.

November 9, 2020

SlovakiaÕs government has tested 4.5 million people out of its 5.5 million population for COVID-19 in the past 2 weeks using rapid antigen tests. The tests are voluntary and free of charge. Just over 1%, or 38,000, of the tests administered so far have come back positive. The government declared a state of emergency earlier this month in response to this surge in infections. Secondary schools have switched to remote learning. All mass events, including church services, are banned. Fitness centres and pools are closed and restaurants can only serve clients outdoors.

November 2, 2020

SlovakiaÕs government has tested two thirds of its population Ñ 3.625 out of 5.5 million people Ñ for COVID-19 using rapid antigen tests. The country plans to test at least another 1 million people in the coming week. The tests are voluntary and free of charge. Just over 1% Ñ or 38,000 Ñ of the tests administered so far have come back positive. A single-day record of 3,363 new cases of COVID-19 were confirmed on October 30, followed by 2,573 the next day. The government declared a state of emergency earlier this month in response to this surge in infections. Secondary schools have switched to remote learning, all mass events, including church services, are banned, fitness centres and pools are closed, and restaurants can only serve clients outdoors.

October 26, 2020

SlovakiaÕs government has begun the process of testing the entire country for COVID-19, with the four most affected provinces having already been tested between October 23 and 25. Slovak health authorities have acquired 13 million rapid antigen tests for the testing of everyone aged 10-65. The tests will be voluntary and free of charge. 2,581 new cases of COVID-19 were confirmed on October 22, just two days after the previous record of 2,202. The government declared a state of emergency earlier this month in response to this surge in infections. Secondary schools have switched to remote learning, all mass events, including church services, are banned, fitness centres and pools are closed, and restaurants can only serve clients outdoors.

October 19, 2020

Slovakia announced on October 17 that the country will test every resident aged 10 and above for COVID-19 in the coming weeks. A pilot run in certain areas is scheduled for the October 25 weekend, while the majority of testing will take place in late October and early November. The government declared a state of emergency earlier this month in response to a surge in infections and introduced new restrictions earlier this week. Secondary schools have switched to remote learning, all mass eventsÑincluding church servicesÑare banned, fitness centres and pools are closed, and restaurants can only serve clients outdoors. On October 17, the health ministry reported nearly 2,000 new confirmed cases, bringing the total to more than 28,000. On October 17, around 500 people gathered outside the government building in downtown Bratislava to protest the latest anti-virus restrictions, despite a ban on gatherings of more than six people.

October 13, 2020

Slovakia is expected to announce new restrictions on October 15 in order to contain the spread of COVID-19, including a requirement to wear face masks in public, and limits to public gatherings. The country has reported a sharp increase in new coronavirus cases in recent days, recording a total of 19,851 cases as of October 11 - up from 13,139 in just 1 week. All public events will be banned, and public gatherings will be limited to six people. Restaurants will only be allowed to serve food outdoors or provide takeout. All fitness facilities, swimming pools and saunas will be closed. Shopping centers will be required to provide at least 15 square meters (160 square feet) of space for each customer, while grocery stores and drugstores will have certain times reserved for senior citizens.

October 5, 2020

Along with Czech, the Slovak government has declared a national state of emergency for at least 45 days, starting on October 1, due to a sustained increase in COVID-19 infections over the past few weeks. The new measures include prohibiting many outdoor gatherings including all family, sports, cultural, and religious events. Exceptions to the mandate include weddings, baptisms, and funerals, and sporting competitions provided that all involved test negative for COVID-19 in the preceding 12 hours. Additionally, all bars, restaurants, and clubs are required to close at 22:00.

September 28, 2020

Slovakia reported a single-day increase of 552 cases on September 26 up from 419 cases reported the previous day, constituting single-day increase records of infections 4 days in a row. The countryÕs death toll remains the lowest in Europe with 44 in total since March.

September 22, 2020

From September 14 until at least October 1, wearing a mask will be mandatory in all public indoor places -- with some exceptions. Slovakian authorities have decided to remove the Czech Republic from its list of coronavirus-safe countries, while adding Bulgaria, Canada, and Sweden to the list. EU citizens from high-risk countries who wish to enter Slovakia will be obliged to present a negative COVID-19 test, or quarantine for 14 days upon arrival in the country. Effective September 18, it is recommended that all retail and service establishments provide a distance of one customer per 10m in their sales areas and that customers maintain a distance of 2 metres between each other at all times.

September 10, 2020

On September 5, Slovakia reported a record single-day increase of 226 new cases of COVID-19. Slovakia's death rate from COVID-19 remains one of the lowest in Europe: 37 fatalities in total since the start of the pandemic. In order to manage the spread of COVID-19, as of September 10, people who return to Slovakia by air, and have visited a high-risk country during the previous 14 days, are obliged to register electronically by filling an online form.Slovak citizens who return from Ukraine by car are also obliged to register electronically. Upon arrival, travellers should be able to prove to the police their successful registration.

August 24, 2020

In anticipation of the upcoming school year and an expected “second wave” of COVID-19, Slovakia has announced a number of new measures that will come into effect on September 1. With respect to schooling – which will resume across the country on September 1 – parents will be required to sign an affidavit regarding their child’s health status upon starting school, including information on whether they have been abroad or participated in a mass event since August 17. Masks will be mandatory for the first 2 weeks of school for secondary school students. Primary school students will be required to wear masks only outside of the classroom (i.e. in hallways). From September 1 to October 1, outdoor mass events will be limited to 1000 people. Indoor events will be limited to 500 people and will only be allowed to run until 11 pm (with the exception of weddings).

August 17, 2020

Amid a resurgence in COVID-19 infections, Slovakia is beginning preparations for the expected “second wave” of the virus. Part of these preparations includes the Health Ministry’s decision to allocate a total of 3503 hospital beds around the country – about 233 beds per hospital – for COVID-19 patients in anticipation of a second outbreak this coming fall.

August 10, 2020

On August 6, Slovakia reported 63 new cases of COVID-19 -- its largest daily increase in new infections since late April. It also recorded its first death since mid-May.

August 4, 2020

The Public Health Office of the Slovak Republic has strongly recommended that Slovak citizens avoid travelling to the regions of Catalonia, Aragon and Navarre in Spain, and the regions of Zagreb and Vukovar-Srijem County in Croatia. Slovak officials noted that the epidemiological situation is less risky in other regions of Spain and Croatia. Both countries currently remain on Slovakia’s list of “less risky” countries.

July 27, 2020

Bulgaria and Montenegro have remained on Slovakia’s list of countries from which travel is deemed unsafe, while travellers from over 30 countries – both inside and outside of the EU – are able to enter Slovakia without restrictions.

July 20, 2020

On July 17, Bulgaria and Montenegro were removed from Slovakia’s list of low-risk countries from which travellers can enter Slovakia with limited restrictions. This means that travellers looking to enter Slovakia from these countries, as well as any others considered high-risk, must self-isolate until they are able to submit a negative COVID-19 test.

July 13, 2020

On July 9, Slovakia’s Ministry of Health reported 53 new cases of COVID-19 – the country’s largest number of new cases in a day since April 22. Following this spike in cases, Prime Minister Igor Matovic announced that the government will meet on July 13 in order to discuss whether to tighten restrictions aimed at preventing the spread of the virus. These restrictions may include a legal amendment requiring that those returning to Slovakia from high-risk countries could be contacted by text message and required to report to Slovak authorities.

July 6, 2020

Following the end of its nationwide state of emergency on June 13, the Slovak government has continued easing restrictions related to COVID-19. Effective July 1, events over 1000 people are permitted and sports stadiums can hold events at 50% capacity.

June 26, 2020

Following the end of its nationwide state of emergency on June 13, the Slovak government has focused primarily on easing restrictions at its borders. The list of “safe” countries whose citizens may enter Slovakia without restriction has been expanded to include Poland, Hungary, and the Czech Republic.

June 19, 2020

On the recommendation of its Ministry of Health, the Slovak government ended its nationwide state of emergency on June 13. On June 10, Slovakia fully reopened its borders to the citizens and permanent residents of 19 countries, including: Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Greece, Hungary and Croatia. Travellers looking to enter Slovakia from other countries are required to: submit a negative RT-PCR test for COVID-19 taken within the last 96 hours upon arrival; remain in self-isolation for minimum 14 days; and take an additional test for COVID-19 on the fifth day of isolation. Nightclubs are permitted to reopen and the limit on mass gatherings is increased to 500 participants for both indoor and outdoor venues. On June 15, restrictions on the number of children allowed to participate in kindergarten and primary school classes was abolished, while students at all other levels will be allowed to return to school on a voluntary basis by the end of June.

June 12, 2020

On June 1, the Slovak government proceeded to the fifth stage of its plan for easing restrictions related to COVID-19. While kindergartens and daycares were already permitted to reopen, primary schools are now also allowed to resume teaching for grades one to five. On June 3, secondary schools were allowed to reopen for final examinations. All retail and catering establishments have been allowed to reopen fully, provided that they are able to maintain a safe distance between patrons and adhere to a strict sanitary regime. All shopping centres, theatres, cinemas, spas, swimming pools and gyms are allowed to reopen according to similar standards. General guidelines on hygiene and physical distancing, such as the obligatory use of facemasks in all public places, continue to apply to most residents. On May 24, the Slovak government announced the release of its contact tracing app, eKaranténa, available on all Android smartphones. Effective immediately, everyone arriving at Slovakia’s border looking to enter or travel throughout the country is required to download and register for the application. iPhone users will be able to download the application after Apple’s approval.

On May 20th, the Slovak government proceeded to the fourth and final stage of its plan for easing restrictions related to COVID-19.
