On June 8, Phase 3 of Belgium’s unlocking strategy is planned to resume and will relax the current restrictions to allow more than four people to visit each other's homes, permit groups of 20 people to play sports outdoors and allow capacity limits of up to 50 children at summer camps. Nightclubs remain closed, but all bars, restaurants, tourist attractions, camping sites, movie theatres and sports training and matches, will reopen.A Black Lives Matter protest scheduled for June 1 was cancelled after facing criticism that the event disregarded public safety by violating bans on gatherings. In Belgium, all airports are being flagged as high-risk areas for COVID-19 infection by the EASA, the country remains the only EU member state to have all its airports blacklisted. European rail operator, Thalys, will begin gradually resuming its international train services between the Netherlands, Belgium, France and Germany next week.
