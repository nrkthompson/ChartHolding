Chile

February 1, 2021

Chile reported 3,371 new COVID-19 cases and 17 deaths on January 27. Five regions reported a decline in cases over the past week. Between January 28 and 31, the country as a whole reported over 4,000 new cases daily. Approximately 56,759 people in Chile have been vaccinated with their first dose; 10,315 people have been fully vaccinated. A mass vaccination campaign is expected to begin in February. Two million doses of the Sinovac vaccine arrived on January 28 and two million more doses are expected. Eligibility criteria for the vaccine was expanded to anyone over the age of 18. The AstraZeneca-Oxford vaccine is now approved for emergency use in Chile.

January 25, 2021

On January 22, Chile reported 4,956 new COVID-19 cases in the country’s largest daily increase since June, along with 84 deaths in the past day. CoronaVac from China’s Sinovac Biotech company was approved by Chilean officials for emergency use. Chile paid $3.5 million to host a clinical trial of the vaccine and ordered 60 million vaccine doses. Chile is also considering approving the AstraZeneca vaccine for emergency use and has bought 14.4 million doses of the vaccine. Chile also ordered enough Pfizer vaccine doses for 29,000 people. 54,458 people have received the first dose of a COVID-19 vaccine, with 8,364 people fully vaccinated.

January 18, 2021

On January 14, Chile reported 4,177 new COVID-19 cases and 90 deaths in the previous 24 hours. The nationwide curfew between 10 pm and 5 am has been extended. Santiago’s international airport will remain open. Chile was the first South American country to begin its COVID-19 vaccination program. Chile has negotiated deals for vaccines with AstraZeneca, Pfizer, and Sinovac. Chilean authorities are aiming to vaccinate 80% of Chileans in the first half of 2021. The first doses of the Pfizer vaccine are being administered to healthcare workers in Santiago and in the southern part of Chile.

December 21, 2020

On December 18, Chile reported 2,404 new COVID-19 cases in the past day, which is the highest number since July. It also reported 44 deaths. On December 16, the Pfizer COVID-19 vaccine was approved in Chile and officials hope that vaccinations can begin this month for health workers, police, military, and high-risk individuals. ChileÕs Pfizer contract is for ten million doses, which should vaccinate five million people. Chile expects to receive some of the Chinese Sinovac COVID-19 vaccines. Chile expects to get 30 million vaccine doses in 2021 and hopes to vaccinate 20,000 people with the Pfizer vaccine in the first quarter of the new year. Restrictions on movement and gatherings in the Metropolitan area will apply on weekends and will last until 2021. Chile has completed more than six million PCR tests and on December 19 the country conducted 40,000 tests.

December 14, 2020

On December 9, Chile's average positivity rate increased to 5.62%, which is the highest that it has been since October. On December 12, Chile reported 1,807 COVID-19 cases in the past day, 64 additional deaths, 651 people in the ICU, 472 people on ventilators and 61 people in critical condition. The COVID-19 situation in the Santiago Metropolitan area is one of the worst in the country and, due to a rise in cases, general travel outside of Santiago is no longer permitted. Santiago and the Metropolitan area have a weekend and holiday curfew in place to curb the spread. The Arturo Benitez International Airport in Santiago will remain open to tourism.

December 7, 2020

On December 5, Chile reported 1,533 new cases in the previous 24 hours. In anticipation of a spike in cases after the holidays, the health minister proposed a plan to add 1,000 more hospital beds. The government anticipates between 3,000 and 9,000 new cases per day after the holidays. The president extended the state of emergency to allow the government to impose lockdowns and curfews. Mass gatherings will not be permitted across Chile between December 24 at 8 pm and December 25 at 5 am, although social gatherings of up to 30 people will be permitted. Mass gatherings will again be banned between 8 pm on December 31 and 5 am on January 2.

November 30, 2020

On November 28, Chile reported 1,718 new cases and 44 new deaths in the past day. Chile has approximately 9,519 reported active cases. Chile remains in a state of catastrophe with a nighttime curfew. ChileÕs National Tourism Sector is providing a Tourist Trust seal to tourist organizations who follow health measures, government recommendations and other prevention advice to reduce the COVID-19 spread. There are protests in Santiago over access to pension funds for COVID-19 support.

November 23, 2020

On November 22, Chile reported 1,573 new daily cases and 48 new deaths. On November 19, Chile imposed additional restrictions in the south and extended Greater ConcepcionÕs curfew from 8 pm to 5 am. On November 23, Chile reopened its borders to foreigners at the Arturo Merino Benitez Airport. Travellers must have received a negative PCR test in the past 72 hours, have health insurance for COVID-19 and have a Health Passport. From November 23 to December 7, foreigners from high-risk countries must quarantine for two weeks and, while other visitors do not have to quarantine, they must download an app to monitor their location and to track symptoms. Foreigners must follow sanitary measures including masks, curfews and not visiting quarantined communes. Hotels, parks, restaurants, museums and beaches must use biosecurity measures. A November 18 announcement said that theaters could reopen with up to 50% capacity.

November 16, 2020

As of November 11, the COVID-19 positivity rate in Chile was 4.08% nationally and 2% in the capital of Santiago and the Metropolitan region. As of November 14, ChileÕs health ministry reported that the number of cases had decreased in seven regions over the past week and that the number of cases had fallen in ten regions over the past two weeks. The daily number of new cases on November 14 was 1,644 with 39 new deaths. In December, Chile will reopen to non-essential travel tourists after an eight month long closure. Visitors can fly into SantiagoÕs Arturo Merino Benitez International Airport provided they have a negative PCR test within 72 hours of arrival. If tourists satisfy health standards and do not have COVID-19 symptoms, they do not have to quarantine. All travelers must provide their location, will be monitored for two weeks, must follow curfews, and must wear a mask when in cities. ChileÕs Deputy Minister for International Economic Relations and the UKÕs Minister for the Americas hosted a seminar on access to vaccines in Latin America and the Caribbean on November 11.

November 9, 2020

On November 7, Chile reported 1,568 daily new cases and 49 new deaths -- as well as 734 people in the hospital, 584 people on ventilators, and 100 people in critical care. The country remains in a state of catastrophe with a curfew that comes into force at night. AstraZeneca received approval to enter clinical trials for its COVID-19 vaccine in Chile. Chile has conducted the most COVID-19 tests in Latin America and officials claim that it is an important aspect of their COVID-19 traceability plan.

November 2, 2020

On October 29, the daily new number of COVID-19 cases was 1,519 and 86 new deaths Ñ making the total number of cases 507,050 and the total number of deaths 14,118. On October 30, the Chilean PCR test positivity rate for the past 24 hours was 4% after 36,880 PCR tests were conducted. Over 4,269,503 total tests have been conducted throughout the pandemic. Over the last week, the number of new cases fell 9% and fell 6% over the last two weeks. The number of daily new cases also decreased in twelve regions this week. Although Chile is slowly reopening, the nationwide nightly curfew remains. By the end of October, approximately 6% of schools had opened or were opening. The government released the National Tourism Plan to share protocols and health recommendations for the tourism sector to reopen. The goals of the plan are reactivating the sector using protocols and health recommendations, economically supporting small business and promoting national tourism. The government also presented a plan that focused on securing enough hospital beds for COVID-19 patients.

October 26, 2020

As of October 24, Chile reported more than 500,000 total COVID-19 cases. The Magallanes region accounts for many of the cases, although their incidence rate has fallen. As of October 20, the number of new cases fell 8% in the last week and 15% in the last two weeks. On October 22, over the last 24 hours Chile had 1,495 new cases and 73 additional fatalities. Despite the quarantine ending in some areas, Chile remains in a state of emergency. Throughout the pandemic, Chile completed over 4 million PCR tests and the health minister suggests that approximately 20% of Chileans have been tested meaning they have a rate of approximately 207,000 tests per million people. In preparation for the October 25 referendum, authorities introduced health measures that included extending voting hours, disinfecting tables, chairs and furniture, and asking voters to come with a pen.

October 19, 2020

On October 16, ChileÕs minister of health announced that the number of deaths had fallen in 13 regions and that the number of new cases had fallen 7% in the last week, 9% in the last two weeks. On October 16, there had been 1,689 new cases and 95 additional deaths in the last 24 hours bringing the cumulative total to 488,190 cases, and 13,529 deaths. Biobio, Los Lagos, Aysen and Tarapaca have had the highest increase in new cases. On October 19, 6 Metropolitan municipalities progressed to phase four, the second last phase of reopening, for the first time. Phase four reopens additional business like cinemas, theaters, cafes and restaurants at 25% capacity, but keeps clubs, pubs and gyms closed.

October 13, 2020

On October 8, Chile reported 1,575 new cases and 77 new deaths, bringing their national totals to 476,016 cases and 13,167 deaths. On October 12, elderly care homes in communities that are not in quarantine and are in at least phase two, may have visitors up to two times per week with up to two visitors per resident, so long as visitors have a health passport. As of October 6, the Magallenes region was considered a high alert threat to the ministry of health because of a possible second wave due to a rate of over 100 cases per 100,000 people. In response, exterior access to the region is restricted, additional medical personnel are available, more patients will be referred to Santiago, and the cause of this increasing rate of infections is being investigated.

October 5, 2020

Chile authorized two clinical studies for the vaccines developed by Biotech CO., Ltd. and Janssen Pharmaceutical Companies. ChileÕs new national budget shared by the president allocates approximately 9 billion pesos to the health budget for vaccine purchase, shorter wait times prior to consultations, surgeries, and primary health care. The country will also continue with their plan to construct 54 hospitals and 120 clinics and to support the Health Mind Program which was designed to combat unhealthy eating and inactive lifestyles. The government designed a new program that dispatches teams into communities to educate people about COVID-19 risks and ways people can protect themselves. In order to respond to the need for economic reactivation, and to support job creation, the president announced an employment subsidy plan. On October 1, five schools reopened in the Pirque community. Teachers and representatives protested reopening schools because the Mayor did not consult the return in person and teachers felt pressured to return.

September 28, 2020

As of September 23, ChileÕs number of confirmed cases has fallen 13% over the last seven days and 7% over the last 14 days. When trying to cross between regions that are at different stages of ChileÕs reopening plan, Chileans must have a permit authorized by the Virtual Police Station and must have a sanitary passport. Chile entered into an agreement with COVAX, the WHO and Pfizer-BioNtech laboratory led international initiative which was created to assist lower income countries with access to COVID-19 vaccines. Chile conveyed an interest in purchasing approximately 14.4 million doses of the Astra-Zeneca-Oxford COVID-19 vaccine when it is developed. The government has also partnered with different international laboratories throughout the course of COVID-19 vaccine development. The president has said that once there is a safe and efficient vaccine, the people most vulnerable to COVID-19 must have prioritized access to the vaccine. While trying to procure vaccines, the president is also focusing on developing ChileÕs R&D, clinical trials and collaborations with laboratories that report promising vaccines. As of September 23, 12 municipalities are moving to phase three of reopening, while 4 municipalities from Southern Chile begin quarantine.

September 22, 2020

As of September 13, Chile’s national PCR positivity rate fell from 17% to 6%. As of September 13, the positivity rate for the Metropolitan region remained around 4%. Over the September 18 national holidays, Chile intended to deploy additional inspectors to ensure compliance with COVID-19 regulations in the Metropolitan region. A collaboration between the Chilean Ministry of Women and Gender Equity, UN Women and Entel led to the development of a mobile survey called the Rapid Assessment Survey, which will be used to determine the impact of COVID-19 on Chileans. Similar surveys have been conducted in Europe and Asia, but Chile is the first Latin American country to conduct the survey.

September 10, 2020

As of September 2, Chile was fourth globally in the number of confirmed COVID-19 cases per million people and was second globally in the number of deaths per million people. Chile wants to offer a free COVID-19 vaccine in 2021. Chile remains closed to tourism and the government expects to release a reopening plan for tourism sectors soon. A September 4 report said that five areas in the Metropolitan region and two areas in the Valparaiso region progressed into the Preparation Stage, or stage three, of the Paso a Paso reopening plan -- where people could go outside and restaurants and cafes are permitted to serve people on sidewalks and terraces. On September 2, bars and restaurants reopened terrace service in some parts of Santiago at 25% capacity. The majority of schools remained closed to in-person activity as of September 2.

August 24, 2020

As of August 19, Chile and Peru were reported as having the worst deaths per capita in Latin America. Santiago continues to reopen slowly: restaurants, bars and clubs remain closed, but people are permitted to go for walks. Fears are rising in Santiago that people are not socially distancing and that, if this continues, the city’s reopening will be short-lived.

August 17, 2020

Case and fatality rates have been declining in Chile over the past two months. On August 11, it was reported that in 24 hours, 39 people had died from COVID-19. The Airbus Foundation, the Chilean Red Cross, Servicios Aereos Kipreos and Servicios Aereos SumaAir have transported over 600kg of medical supplies and PPE to Chile. Students in the San Fernando commune will not return to in-person classes this year for their safety. On August 17, Chile lifted its quarantine on Santiago--one of the longest quarantines in the world--and moved the city into the transitional phase, or phase two of reopening. The government is working to verify that companies in the Mayor of Providencia region adhere to COVID-19 health measures. Chile’s government will establish a forum for people to anonymously report companies that do not comply with COVID-19 health measures.

August 10, 2020

On August 6, Chile’s minister of health reported a decrease in COVID-19 cases nationally. He said that cases had decreased drastically in 11 regions over the last seven days, and in eight regions over the last two weeks. However, Punta Arenas regressed from phase three of reopening to phase two, following a 200% increase in new cases over the past week. The government anticipates that their Paso-a-Paso reopening plan will remain in effect until the end of November. The Chilean government is working on gradually reopening national parks with restrictions. These include: limiting groups, short safety presentations, encouraging credit card payment, and encouraging visitors to sanitize personal items before and after visiting. The Alerce Costero National Park in the Los Rios region reopened on August 5. 62 other national protected areas are expected to reopen as municipalities enter phases two to five of reopening. The government published a document with recommendations for employers and employees to return to work safely. The document recommends: staying informed; consulting labour unions and creating health and safety committees with experts; increasing awareness and training; adapting the physical workspace; mental health support; traceability; and continuously evaluating and updating their plans. A sanitary residence has also been created for pregnant and lactating women who test COVID-19 positive. The government will financially support a COVID-19 vaccine clinical trial by Universidad Catolica and Sinovac that is entering phase three of clinical trials. Authorities suggest that the vaccine could be ready by 2021.

August 4, 2020

On July 28, Chile began gradually lifting lockdown restrictions in Santiago. As part of the Chilean government’s Testing Traceability and Isolation strategy, the Undersecretary of Public Health and Mayor of Las Condes conducted an Active Search procedure on July 29. This procedure performs PCR testing on pedestrians in communes that have entered the Transition phase, or phase two, of reopening. Las Condes was one of the communes that entered phase two and which began daily testing in communes. The government also expects people to prove residency if they are moving around in communes in the Transition phase. The Undersecretary for Children and the Undersecretary for Public Health met with the Chilean Society of Pediatrics and other health experts to determine how permits should work for children and adolescents to leave home during the pandemic and when and where they should be permitted to go. The government’s decision to reopen has been criticized by medical experts in Chile who claim that the country is not ready to reopen.

July 27, 2020

The government is promoting two ventilators that were developed in Chile as part of their COVID-19 response. The government also released a program called Territorial Viewer to show infections per square kilometer, quarantines, sanitary cordons and other indicators related to the presence of COVID-19 in Chile. The viewer will help the government identify areas that require attention and areas where PCR should be prioritized. In a July 21 report, President Pinera explained that reopening would proceed gradually, consider community opinions, incorporate WHO recommendations and consider district and regional differences -- particularly regarding regional and district abilities to comply with health criteria. Pinera also suggested that Chile would allow areas to reintroduce lockdown measures if necessary. The first phase of reopening is “quarantine”; it limits mobility, requires permits for movement, and closes gyms, classes, theaters and more. The second phase is “transitional”; it allows for increased movement but includes quarantines on weekends and some other limitations. While the government released its reopening plan on July 19 and said that the situation had improved in 12 regions, it did not reveal when Santiago and northern mining regions would reopen. As of July 27, 65 communities and territories were in the quarantine phase, zero were in the transition phase, 266 were in the preparation phase and 22 were in the initial opening phase. On July 28, Chile’s capital will ease some lockdown restrictions. The government will allow children and people 75 years and older to leave home.

July 20, 2020

As of July 14, there were approximately 453 ventilators available and 5,771 patients in the 154 sanitary residences—with 10,088 vacancies remaining. Chile has reported a drop in positive cases; the past week showed a 15% drop in new cases and the past two weeks showed a 36% drop. The rate of positive PCR tests is decreasing; it was 15% as of July 14. The government advises Chileans to be cautious in Aysen and Los Rios, where confinement began ending on July 13 and reopening is voluntary. On July 19, Chile released a five stage reopening plan from total quarantine to advanced reopening that will be based on epidemiological criteria, healthcare capacity and case traceability for each territory. The first phase is quarantine with limited mobility, permits for movement, and closure of gyms, classes, theaters and more. The second phase is transitional with increased movement, but includes quarantines on weekends and some other limitations. Professional soccer players, referees and high-performance athletes such as athletes training for the Olympics will have collective permits with capacity limits that allow teams in areas under quarantine to resume training. During the week of July 6, a record of approximately 15,410 people were detained for health regulation violations.

July 13, 2020

On July 6, Chile had the sixth-most COVID-19 patients globally: 3000 new cases and 76 dead in 24 hours. The innovative PPE that won an April COVID-19 innovation challenge and a newly designed ventilator will be tested in health centers. Latin American and Caribbean representatives, the IMF and the World Bank are collaborating on pandemic response. From July 7 to 21, the government implemented a sanitary cordon in la Tirana to prevent a religious festival. In urban and populated areas, masks will be mandatory in public. The government wants to increase PCR testing abilities in the metropolitan area. In response to a 20% decline in the rate of positive COVID-19 test results between June 16 and July 8, the government will announce a national reopening plan. Until then, reopening will only be in places with few cases over a prolonged period, low incidence, strong contact tracing, no increase in cases and a good healthcare system. The metropolitan areas are among the most affected parts of Chile; they are seeing a decrease in cases and are working on a gradual reopening plan that educates Chileans and requires masks and social distancing. On July 13, five of 22 sanitary protection measures will be lifted in Aysen and Los Rios. These measures will reopen cinemas and restaurants at 25% capacity, non-elective surgeries, sports events with 10 participants indoors or 50 participants outdoors, and allow adults over the age of 75 to go outside with permits. A sanitary cordon that separates these regions was installed on July 10. An epidemiologist said that COVID-19 rates in Chile do not support relaxing health measures and that the country should wait until it has a 10% positivity rate and fewer cases before reopening.

July 6, 2020

On June 30, Chile reported over 3,300 new cases in one day--with 389 people in critical condition and 293 ventilators still available. As of June 30, Chile had conducted 1,109,792 PCR tests. Chile is beginning to see a decline in its COVID-19 positivity rate. On June 26, additional municipalities entered quarantine. Cordons sanitaires were enforced for the June 27 long weekend in some areas. Requests for temporary permits to leave home during quarantine have decreased. On June 26, Chile received 82 new ventilators and PPE was sent to laboratories that assess PCR tests. The Ministry of Health is introducing a new platform to improve contact tracing capabilities and data collection for health care professionals. The Chilean government is funding 63 research projects on COVID-19 and its impact on social structures like education and work. The government will also support engineering technology projects for AI and prediction. On July 1, middle school students in Rapa Nui (Easter Island) returned in person; on July 8 basic education will resume and on July 15 kindergarten will resume. The number of people detained for violating curfew saw a small decrease.

June 26, 2020

As of June 19, the Santiago metropolitan region was ranked the fourth area globally for the most COVID-19 cases. The country was ranked ninth for the most COVID-19 cases and 20th for the most deaths. On June 19 and 24, additional quarantines were implemented. On June 19, additional restrictive measures were implemented in some quarantined areas. The health minister does not want a complete hibernation because of the need to satisfy essential services, food needs, the needs of seniors and medical needs, therefore they are exploring escalating lockdown measures to increase compliance. Some of the stricter measures include reducing the number of permits for movement from five to two weekly, offering permits for people to make funeral and medical preparations, asking essential companies to reduce the number of people working in person and others. Critics describe that while the government prepared health materials early in the outbreak, they did not account for poverty: the difficulty of people isolating in dense cities, underlying health conditions, the need for people to work so that they can buy food, and a lack of access to (high-speed) internet.

June 19, 2020

Chile reported the ninth most cases in the world. The Minister of Health anticipates that June will be Chile’s most difficult month during the pandemic and anticipates that 13% of the population will be hospitalized. Between May 11 and June 9, a record of 6,498 Chileans died, which is the most deaths in any one month over the past ten years. GenoSur, a Chilean biotechnology firm, is working with an American company in Miami to produce sampling collection kits and transport devices for COVID-19. As of June 12, additional quarantine measures were introduced in urban areas while quarantines were extended in other regions. A minister confirmed that the country’s state of emergency will be extended for another 90 days, with continued sanitary controls, curfews and army presence. Between June 11 and 12, fewer Chileans were detained for curfew violations. Amid rising new daily cases and death tolls, and scandals about COVID-19 case reporting discrepancies, Minister of Health Jaime Manalich resigned and was replaced by Enrique Paris. An Activa Research Citizen Pulso poll suggests that the president’s approval ratings rose to 13.6% while his cabinet’s rejection ratings fell to 79.2% compared to May’s poll results.

June 12, 2020

Chile has one of the highest numbers of COVID-19 cases in Latin America and to ensure comparability and accuracy in their count, they will now include information found on death certificates. After approximately 76 days under partial or total lockdown, Chile’s hospitals are close to collapsing. 75 people, two of whom had tested positive for COVID-19, were detained and face fines for violating lockdown regulations. Chile is making permits in regions that are under lockdown stricter to deter the abuse of permits and will be extending lockdowns in Chile. Chile is increasing the number of hospital transport vans, the number of critical care beds in hospitals and the number of sanitary residences used for self-isolation. The government donated masks, gloves and sanitizer to a group of taxi drivers in the Metropolitan Region. The government supported the University of Chile’s launch of a free traceability course based on the John Hopkins contact tracing course. On July 31, the National Professional Football Association intends to resume football games after three to four weeks of training camp.

June 5, 2020

Chile has over 100,000 cases and had a new peak of daily deaths from COVID-19 on June 2 with 75. Santiago and 38 towns in the area continue to be under lockdown. Chile received 65 more ventilators on May 29. To meet a government goal of increased hospital patient capacity, hospitals are converting other departments to accommodate more beds and more ventilators. The Ministry of Health is also trying to increase their capacities in testing, patient treatment and isolation with more sanitary residences. Through public-private collaborations, more PPE will be delivered to nursing homes. Major cities in the country remain under lockdown. As of Friday May 29, Chile reported their highest number of deaths in one day at 49 deaths, the government expects the rising trend to continue without proper distancing and lockdown measures being respected. The government released a National Tourism Plan to offer support and guidance for protocols in restimulating the tourism sector.

May 29, 2020

Chile is currently experiencing growing numbers of daily cases and on May 25th had 3709 new daily cases. In Latin America, Chile has the third most COVID-19 patients with approximately 82,200 cases and 840 dead. Chile is consequently facing speculations of a collapsing health care system where treatment is becoming more selective between patients that can and cannot be saved. This distinction between patients is growing in Santiago.Between May 23-27, Chile received more ventilators and PPE. Chile began integrating high flow oxygen therapy into their treatment plans and is teaching this therapy in universities. The government implemented new health measures that included converting more beds to accommodate a higher ICU capacity and increasing sanitary residences to facilitate quarantining for Chileans. To further increase health care capacity by 500 beds, Chile is creating 5 modular hospitals, with the first one expected to be ready in June. Despite plans to begin reopening borders, schools, and workplaces in May, a large portion of the country remains closed. The President reduced the salaries of authorities and public officials and Congress was granted permission to operate virtually.

May 22, 2020

All of Santiago is now under quarantine due to a recent uptick in cases; this is an expansion of the country’s earlier strategy to use dynamic or selective quarantines, as opposed to quarantining entire cities. Further restrictions were imposed on quarantined regions, including Santiago, where people now have five permits to go outside weekly, only two of which can be used on the supermarket. Children cannot go between their parents’ households, if they live separately, and there will be no more permits to change addresses. Approximately 33,000 people have been detained for violating curfews and approximately 1,401 people have been detained for violating health restrictions. As of May 21st, Chile registered its highest number of daily deaths at 45. Chile received more ventilators from Holland and China. The use of hyperimmune plasma treatment, which uses antibodies from a recovered patient to treat someone who is still ill, had its first success in Chile at curing a COVID-19 patient. Chilean officials expressed that they do not want to relax restrictions until there is a consistent two to three week long drop in cases and instead will focus on increasing treatment capacity. Clashes between protestors and police erupted over food shortages in Santiago.
