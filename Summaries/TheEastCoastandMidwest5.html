After weeks of lobbying, on June 15 personal-care businesses, such as hair and nail salons, were allowed to reopen in Michigan, the last state that kept those businesses closed.7 Governor Gretchen Whitmer has extended the state of emergency as Michigan is on track to contain COVID-19, but not out of the woods yet.8 Illinois Governor JB Pritzker announced on June 17 a $900 million financial relief package across ten programs, including rental assistance and an evictions ban through the summer.9 On June 18, Governor Andrew Cuomo announced that New York City is on track to enter phase 2 of reopening on June 22, as the number of confirmed cases continue to decrease (even though the number of active cases continues to gradually increase).10 This will allow restaurants and bars to open for outdoor service, along with offices, indoor retail, hair salons and barbershops, with hygienic conditions.11 On June 17, Maine Governor Janet Mills announced that the three remaining counties were allowed to reopen bars or restaurants for outdoor service, as well as gyms, with face coverings encouraged, but not yet mandatory.12
