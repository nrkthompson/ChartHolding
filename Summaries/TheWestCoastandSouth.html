The West Coast and South

June 19, 2020

The number of COVID-19 cases is increasing in the South and West. On June 11, the Governor of Washington released a plan to reopen schools fall 2020-2021. In fall, 15-20% of UCLA classes will be either in-person or hybrid. Dr. Fauci explained that an uptick in cases as states open is less concerning than the increase in hospitalizations, which approximately six states are experiencing with reopening. On Friday June 12, Texas and North Carolina reported the highest hospitalization numbers. The governor of Texas explained that Texas would continue reopening since despite their hospitalization numbers, hospital beds remain available. California will allow food trucks with sanitary restrictions. The Director of Health in Orange County, California, resigned after protests at her home and death threats against her for making the use of masks mandatory in May. Case numbers are rising in Florida, and some models predict that it could be a new large epicenter of the virus in the US.

June 12, 2020

As Florida, Texas and other states reopen, they are seeing a rise in COVID-19 cases; as of June 9 Texas reported two consecutive days of record breaking COVID-19 hospitalizations. In Washington, counties range from phase 1 to 3, and as of June 8 employees are required to wear masks with exceptions for individuals while customers are also encouraged to do the same. In addition to 110 million surgical masks already sent, California is receiving 150 million N95 masks from BYD North America. California is in phase 2 of reopening and on June 12 permitted movie theaters, bowling alleys and some other entertainment facilities to reopen with capacity limits and the recommended use of reservations and barriers. Other high risk businesses like some of the entertainment facilities not mentioned above remain closed in California. In California and Kentucky, Amazon is developing labs to test their staff, and considering more temperature checks and pulse oximetry screening, which measures oxygen levels in the blood. On June 11, Orlando SeaWorld, Aquatica and Discovery Cove reopened with reservations and sanitary precautions. With social distancing, masks and other sanitary measures, NASCAR will welcome fans at races in June including at the Dixie Vodka 400 on June 14 in Florida and at the GEICO 500 on June 21in Alabama.

June 5, 2020

More counties in Washington entered phase 2 and reopened outdoor recreation, dine-in restaurants, gyms and personal care services. Washington DC will remain in phase 1 following an increase in cases on June 1. After forming a public-private partnership with Home Depot and Publix, Florida will open more testing sites. Texas enters phase 3 with larger gatherings and more activities. Only some Texas school districts decided to reopen. In response to the George Floyd protests, California and Florida have temporarily closed some testing sites.

May 29, 2020

The FDA has authorized COVID-19 saliva tests. Dr. Fauci asserted that current reopening plans can influence the occurrence of a potential second wave. The CDC discourages using antibody tests as a guide for grouping people in schools, dormitories, and correctional facilities. The US banned arrivals of people who had been to Brazil within 14 days prior to arrival. The US greatly reduced their distribution of visas in April compared to last year. Texas suspended in-person county and municipal jail visitations. Texas is also expanding phase two reopening to include movie theatres, water parks, adult recreation activities, and mall food courts. Despite recent spikes in cases, Washington will not restart their evaluation period before progressing in their reopening. Some counties in Washington are in phase one with others in phase two. Washington DC will lift their stay home order, increase permitted non-essential services, and allow contact services with appointments, but museums and theatres will not open yet. In Florida, Disney World, SeaWorld, and Universal Studios will open between June 11th and July 15th. Florida is lifting restrictions on youth activities. California launched the California Connected contact tracing app where features provide COVID-19 positive patients with health care recommendations. The DHS may implement temperature checks at airports and will change policies in airports to limit contact. Several public spaces reported large groups of people visiting beaches, speedways, and other places over the Memorial Day long weekend. Montgomery Alabama’s mayor suggests a false sense of security from reopening influenced some of the crowds. Houston authorities received complaints of restaurants and bars violating capacity limits. Nancy Pelosi and Chuck Schumer denounced President Trump’s testing and supply provisions as inadequate.

May 22, 2020

Navajo Nation will impose a 57 hour lockdown this weekend. Tensions are rising in Alabama after Montgomery reported an ICU bed shortage. Alabama, North Dakota, and South Carolina implemented the Apple and Google bluetooth based digital contact tracing application. All 50 US states are now partially reopened. Now in phase two, between May 18th and 31st, Texas will be opening more recreational areas, professional sports without spectators, and daytime youth camps. On June 1st, public schools can offer in-person summer school programs pending health regulations. Cases in Texas are rising as the state reopens, but the healthcare system claims not to be overwhelmed. Approximately ten Washington counties expanded reopening on May 18th to include personal-care businesses, dine-in restaurants, retail stores, and gyms. 1200 California pastors will defy restrictions and resume services on May 31st, before entering Stage 3. Public health official, Rick Bright, made allegations of law violations, mismanagement, wasted funds, abuse of power, danger to public health, and censorship during his testimony. In response to the handling of COVID-19 and Chinese involvement, President Trump threatened to end American WHO funding and withdraw from America’s WHO partnership.
